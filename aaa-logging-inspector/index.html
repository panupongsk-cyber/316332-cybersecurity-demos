AAA Logging & Accountability Inspector

Analyze audit log details under different security setups. Determine who is responsible for the leaked attendee database at 10:15:00.

🔎 Incident Scenario

At exactly 10:15:00, the Campus Club Event Roster database was downloaded and leaked online. You must select a logging level, review the console stream, and answer who is accountable.

⚙️ Log Configuration

1. Shared Admin Account All officers share a single 'admin' credential. Low accountability overhead.
2. Individual Accounts (No Role Boundaries) Every volunteer gets their own login, but everyone is granted full export access.
3. Role-Based Access Controls (AAA) Individual logins, strict least-privilege checks, and mandatory MFA before exports.

💡 Classroom Walkthrough Script & Discussion

Use this demo to illustrate Authentication, Authorization, and Accountability:

  • Level 1 (Shared Account): Examine the logs. At 10:15:00, user 'admin' downloaded the file. Because everyone shared the password, it is impossible to identify the human. Select "Unknown" in the Verdict box to see the GRC failure feedback.
  • Level 2 (No Role Boundaries): Select Level 2. The logs now show individual logins. At 10:15:00, user 'charlie' downloaded the file. We have **Accountability**! But wait—Charlie is a regular member. Why does he have authorization to export the database? Select "Charlie" and observe the **Authorization/Least Privilege** design failure.
  • Level 3 (True AAA): Select Level 3. Charlie attempts the export but is **BLOCKED** by RBAC (Authorization check). Alice, the President, exports it after passing an **MFA challenge**. We have authenticated identity, verified authorization, and clear audit evidence. Select "Alice" to view the success explanation.
